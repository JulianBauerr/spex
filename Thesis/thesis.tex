\section{Qubit Excitation Unitary Operation -- Algebraic Derivation}

Starting from the definition of the qubit excitation unitary in \eqref{eq:qubit_exc_unitary_def}, we can express the Generatro in terms of the ladder operators defined in \eqref{eq:ladder_operators}. To demonstrate this, we expand the constituent terms step-by-step. Expanding the first term, $(\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_+ \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_-)$, yields:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{4}\left( \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x + i \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \right) \left( \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x - i \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y \right) =
  \frac{1}{4} \left(
                \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x -
    i\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y +
    i\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x +
                \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y
  \right).
\end{equation}

Similarly, expanding the second term, $(\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_- \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_+)$, gives:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{4}\left( \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x - i \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \right) \left( \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x + i \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y \right) =
  \frac{1}{4} \left(
                \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x +
    i\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y -
    i\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x +
                \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y
  \right).
\end{equation}

The definition of the generator requires subtracting these two expressions. Upon subtraction, the identical real components $\left( \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x \text{and} \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y \right)$cancel out entirely. Only the imaginary terms remain, reducing the final expression to:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{4}
  \left(
    -2i\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y +
  2i\hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x
  \right) =
  - \frac{i}{2}
  \left(
    \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_x \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_y -
    \hat{\sigma}^{(b)}_y \hat{\sigma}^{(i)}_x
  \right).
\end{equation}
The $-\frac{i}{2}$ gets dropped for simplicity.
